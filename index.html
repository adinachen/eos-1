1 Person, 7 Billion Impact

Living day by day in our busy lives, it is easy to feel immortal, untouchable, even ephemeral. What does it matter that we may throw a wrapper onto the street as we walk by. However, our isolated actions culminate and multiply in impact, spanning the world and cutting years off of our futures.

For better or worse, one person among the seven billion people inhabiting the Earth can make an impact.

Volunteer Sites

    Tule Ponds
    Tule Ponds is a training center for students adults interesting in attaining restoration skills. It is based in Fremont, California and serves as a home to several forms of wildlife. Volunteers may help by planting special flora and fauna intended to replace harmful effects from invasive species. Volunteers may also perform other tasks such as mulching and removing invasive species.

    Lake Cunningham
    Lake Cunningham welcomes volunteers looking to help the environment. The lake is located in San Jose, California and involves tasks such as invasive plant removal, mulching, planting, and watering. Most available workdays are on Saturdays. Find out more about how you can contribute by clicking "Lake Cunningham"

  • Our City Forest
    Join Our City Forest's planting team by signing up to volunteer. The organization includes several tasks such as planting, transplanting, maintaining care of trees, shaping land to drought-resistant landscapes. Other volunteering options are available as well. The main office is located in San Jose. Check the link above to find more information about Our City Forest volunteering.

  • Garden for the Environment
    Garden for the Environment is a San Francisco based garden designed to mitigate some hazardous effects of city living. Drop-in garden volunteers are welcomed every Wednesday and Saturday. All ages are welcome and no previous gardening experience is required. Tasks involve composting, planting, pruning, weeding, etc.
